Marsupial gestation overview - Example

Marsupials such as kangaroos and wallabies have an unusually short gestation period and complete most of their development inside the mother pouch after birth.

How the marsupial pouch works

The newborn joey crawls to the pouch and attaches to a teat where it continues to grow for many weeks before it can survive independently.

Kangaroo reproduction facts

A female kangaroo can pause an embryo development in a process called embryonic diapause until conditions are favorable for the joey to grow.
