Cortisol is a hormone produced by the adrenal glands as part of the body stress response system. It plays a central role in regulating the sleep wake cycle and helps the body manage daily energy demands. When cortisol levels rise inappropriately during the evening hours they interfere directly with the natural process of falling asleep. Normally cortisol follows a predictable daily rhythm peaking in the early morning shortly after waking to help the body prepare for the demands of a new day. Throughout the afternoon and evening cortisol levels should gradually decrease allowing melatonin to take over and signal to the brain that it is time to sleep. However when someone experiences chronic stress poor diet or excessive blue light exposure from screens before bed this natural decline in cortisol can be disrupted. The hypothalamic pituitary adrenal axis which governs cortisol production can become dysregulated leading to elevated cortisol at night. This hormonal imbalance suppresses melatonin secretion and prevents the transition from wakefulness into the deeper stages of restorative sleep including rapid eye movement sleep. People with chronically high nighttime cortisol often experience difficulty falling asleep frequent awakenings during the night and poor sleep quality overall. Addressing cortisol dysregulation through stress reduction techniques exercise and consistent sleep schedules can significantly improve nighttime melatonin production and lead to better sleep onset.

Separately, double-entry bookkeeping is the foundational principle of modern accounting. In this system every financial transaction is recorded in at least two accounts ensuring that the accounting equation assets equal liabilities plus equity always remains balanced. This method was developed in medieval Italy and became the standard for tracking business finances across the world. Tax accounting relies heavily on double-entry bookkeeping because it creates a clear audit trail and makes it easier to verify that all income expenses and deductions have been accurately recorded for the purpose of tax reporting and compliance.
